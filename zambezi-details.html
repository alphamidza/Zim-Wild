Zambezi National Park

Matabeleland North, Zimbabwe

About Zambezi National Park

Zambezi National Park is a stunning wildlife reserve located along the Zambezi River upstream from Victoria Falls. Established in 1979 and covering 560 square kilometers, the park offers a spectacular combination of riverine scenery and abundant wildlife. The park forms part of the Kavango-Zambezi Transfrontier Conservation Area (KAZA), the largest transfrontier conservation area in the world.

Wildlife

The park is home to approximately 4,000 buffalo and healthy populations of elephants, lions, leopards, and hippos. The Zambezi River supports large crocodile populations and over 75 mammal species. Birdlife is particularly rich with over 400 species recorded, including the African fish eagle, Pel's fishing owl, and numerous kingfishers and herons.

Unique Features

What makes Zambezi National Park special is its combination of river activities and game viewing. The park offers excellent fishing opportunities (catch-and-release for tigerfish), river cruises, and canoe safaris. The riverine forests along the Zambezi create a lush contrast to the drier inland areas. The park's proximity to Victoria Falls makes it easily accessible for visitors.

Conservation

The park plays a crucial role in protecting the Zambezi River ecosystem. Conservation efforts focus on anti-poaching, particularly for elephants and the vulnerable hippopotamus population. The park is part of important wildlife corridors that allow animals to move between Zimbabwe, Zambia, and Botswana.

Tourism

Visitors can enjoy game drives, walking safaris, river cruises, and fishing. The park has several lodges and campsites with stunning river views. The best time to visit is during the dry season (May to October) when animals congregate near the river. The park offers a more secluded wildlife experience compared to the busier Victoria Falls area.
