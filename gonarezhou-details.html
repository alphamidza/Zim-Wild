Gonarezhou National Park

Masvingo, Zimbabwe

About Gonarezhou National Park

Gonarezhou National Park, meaning "Place of Elephants" in the Shona language, is Zimbabwe's second-largest national park covering 5,053 square kilometers in the southeastern lowveld. Established in 1975, it forms part of the Great Limpopo Transfrontier Park, linking with South Africa's Kruger National Park and Mozambique's Limpopo National Park.

Wildlife

The park is home to approximately 11,000 elephants and significant populations of lions, leopards, and African wild dogs. The park's diverse ecosystems support over 500 bird species, making it one of Zimbabwe's premier birding destinations. Other notable species include buffalo, nyala, eland, and the rare samango monkey.

Unique Features

Gonarezhou is renowned for its dramatic sandstone cliffs, particularly the Chilojo Cliffs which rise 180 meters above the Runde River valley. The park has three major rivers - Save, Runde, and Mwenezi - which create diverse habitats. The park's wilderness character is more rugged and remote than other Zimbabwean parks, offering a true African bush experience.

Conservation

As part of the Great Limpopo Transfrontier Park, Gonarezhou plays a crucial role in regional conservation efforts. The park has implemented successful community-based conservation programs and anti-poaching initiatives. Elephant populations have rebounded significantly since the 1990s, and predator populations are steadily increasing.

Tourism

Gonarezhou offers excellent game viewing with fewer visitors than other parks, providing a more exclusive safari experience. Activities include game drives, walking safaris, and bird watching. The best time to visit is during the dry season (May to October) when animals congregate around water sources. The park has several lodges and campsites, with new luxury options recently developed.
