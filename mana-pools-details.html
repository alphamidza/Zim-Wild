Mana Pools National Park

Mashonaland West, Zimbabwe

About Mana Pools National Park

Mana Pools National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in northern Zimbabwe along the Zambezi River. The name "Mana" means "four" in the local Shona language, referring to the four large permanent pools formed by the meandering river. Covering 2,500 square kilometers, it's one of Zimbabwe's most spectacular wilderness areas.

Wildlife

Mana Pools is home to approximately 12,000 buffalo and significant populations of elephants, lions, leopards, and cheetahs. The park is particularly famous for its large bull elephants that stand on their hind legs to reach higher branches. The floodplains support rich wildlife including over 350 bird species, making it a birdwatcher's paradise.

Unique Features

What sets Mana Pools apart is its walking safari experience - one of the few parks in Africa where visitors can walk unguided (though guided walks are recommended). The park's albida forests create a unique ecosystem where animals gather during the dry season. The Zambezi River forms the northern boundary, offering spectacular views and opportunities for canoe safaris.

Conservation

As a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Mana Pools has special conservation status. The park is part of the Lower Zambezi-Mana Pools Transfrontier Conservation Area, linking with Zambia's protected areas. Anti-poaching efforts focus particularly on elephants and the endangered African wild dog.

Tourism

The park offers unique safari experiences including walking safaris, canoe trips on the Zambezi, and game drives. Accommodation ranges from luxury lodges to basic campsites. The best time to visit is during the dry season (May to October) when wildlife congregates around the water sources.
